## The situation

A scale-up's engineering team was fully consumed by roadmap work while a backlog of integrations, refactors, and long-promised features aged in the tracker. The classic responses both fail: hiring takes quarters, and handing the backlog to an outside agency usually means onboarding overhead that eats the capacity it adds.

## The approach

We embedded a small pod of vetted engineers — every one of them screened by a working engineer through our technical vetting funnel before they ever reached this client — directly into the client's existing workflow. Their sprint board, their repositories, their review standards. No parallel process, no separate tooling, no "vendor portal."

Scope was written before the team was allocated: which backlog areas the pod owns, what "done" means for each, and where the boundary with the core team's roadmap work sits. That document, not a rate card, is what the engagement was priced against.

## The operating rhythm

- **Weekly working demos** — running software shown every week, not status decks.
- **Shared sprint board** — the client sees the same view we do, at all times.
- **One delivery owner** — a single accountable lead on our side, so escalation is a conversation, not a ticket.

## What the client owns

Everything, from the first commit: source, documentation, infrastructure configuration, and IP. Handover is not a project phase at the end — it is the default state throughout, which is what makes it safe for the client to scale the pod down as the backlog burns down.
